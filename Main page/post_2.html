The Evolution of "Video Game Status"

This event shall change it all!

Posted by Uday , Team Inaayat on 10-4-2016

The UK government sets up an international “Olympics of e-sports” this summer! Okay if you are a hardcore gamer , then you may have bounced atleast once after hearing this fantastic news! But if you are someone who dose'nt care about which new game is going to release or what did Cratos do in God of war. You may pick your side but the gaming era is changing and this event which is to begin this summer shall change the face of gaming industry!

The gaming Olympics aka e-Olympics!


"eSports Now Recognized as 2nd-Level Olympic Sport"

On January 27 , the Korean eSport Association (KeSPA) was able to push for the accreditation of eSports as a 2nd-level Olympic sport by the Korean Olympic Committee (KOC).The decision was made by the Korea Sports Council’s board of directors during a conference at the Seoul Olympic Parktel yesterday, Jan. 27, 2015.
The most awaited event which includes the selected few from each country who bash their controllers in order to win it all , is going to happen simultaneously with the the RIO Olympics at Brazil which is dated from 5th August to 21st August.

The Let down

On social media, gamers pushed back on the possibility of seeing video games in the Olympics, stating that e-games shall not be directly compared with physical games. Also only 4 countries namely Britain , Canada , Brazil and the U.S On popular social media sharing site Reddit, League of Legends fans and players of FIFA (16 notably :P) lamented the move by KeSPA(Korean eSport Association), stating that the inclusion of eSports into the Olympics is problematic, owing to the multitude of eSports titles currently in the market.

The games you will get to watch !

The strategy games as well as simulation games , the well know games world wide like
-> FIFA 16
-> Dota 2
-> Need for speed
-> Counter strike

The conclusion

The new eGames will be looking to grab in some reflected glory from the upcoming Rio Olympic Games, hosting its first competition as a two-day exclaimed event in Rio during the Summer Olympics in August. Any nation will be welcome to field an "eTeam" (their term, not ours) in the eGames, but so far only four countries are confirmed as participants: Britain, Canada, Brazil, and the US.
